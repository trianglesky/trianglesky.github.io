About the mandalas

Strictly speaking, mandalas are Hindu/Buddhist images made as part of some kind of ritual. They often represent ideas core to these traditions, such as impermanence and different levels of existence; the sand mandalas created by Buddhist monks are famously swept up and poured in the river.

The idea was taken on by the new age movement, so you now see these kind of drawings used to represent all sorts of things like chakras.

These drawings are symmetrical circles, and even though I call them mandalas, they aren't really made with any Buddhist, Hindu or new age thinking behind them. They're just things I like drawing.

I start by making a grid in pencil with compasses and a set square. Then I work freehand with a pen, starting in the middle and working outwards.

This mandala was used as the artwork for the album Western Skies / Eastern Dreams by the New York based duo Brandon Terzic and Ravi Padmanabha.
